NM SERVICES
Responsible AI & Data Usage Policy

Version: 1.4
Effective Date: January 1, 2026
Applies To: All employees, contractors, vendors, and third-party partners

1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish guidelines for the safe, ethical, and compliant use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) systems within NM Services (“the Company”).
This policy protects customer data, company intellectual property, and regulatory compliance obligations under applicable financial and privacy laws.

2. Definitions
Confidential Data
Any non-public business, customer, employee, or financial information.
Sensitive Personal Information (SPI)

Includes but is not limited to:
Social Security Numbers
Bank account numbers
Credit card numbers
Tax records
Government identification numbers
Health information
Biometric identifiers

Public AI Tools
AI systems not hosted or controlled by NM infrastructure (e.g., external chatbots, public LLM platforms).

3. Acceptable Use of AI
Employees may use approved AI systems for:
Drafting internal documentation
Summarizing non-confidential reports
Code assistance for internal tools
Data analysis on anonymized datasets
Customer support responses using approved templates
All AI-generated content must be reviewed by a human before external distribution.

4. Prohibited Activities
The following actions are strictly prohibited:

4.1 Sharing Sensitive Data
Employees must NOT input any of the following into AI systems:
Customer financial data
Social Security Numbers
Account credentials
API keys or passwords
Internal audit reports
Non-public financial results
Source code of proprietary systems

4.2 Bypassing Security Controls
Attempting to override, jailbreak, manipulate, or trick AI systems into revealing restricted information is considered a security violation.

4.3 Automated Decision Making
AI systems may not independently:
Approve loans
Deny customer accounts
Perform compliance judgments
Generate legal advice
Provide investment recommendations
A qualified employee must always review decisions.

5. Data Classification Rules
Data Type	                       AI Usage Allowed
Public marketing material	              Yes
Internal policies	                      Yes
Customer support templates	              Yes
Customer personal data	                  No
Financial transactions	                  No
Authentication credentials	              No
Regulatory filings (unreleased)	          No

6. Human Oversight Requirement
All Medium-Risk AI outputs must be reviewed by the employee generating them.
High-Risk scenarios require approval from:
AI Governance Team OR Information Security Team
Examples:
Customer communication containing financial advice
Compliance interpretation
Regulatory responses


7. Incident Reporting
Employees must report AI-related incidents immediately if they observe:
Data leakage
Incorrect financial guidance
Hallucinated compliance information
Exposure of internal company systems

Report via:
security@nm.com


8. Violations
Violations may result in:
Access revocation
Disciplinary action
Termination of employment
Legal action where applicable


9. Regulatory Alignment
This policy aligns with:
GLBA (Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act)
SOC2 Security Controls
Internal Data Protection Standard IDS-21
Responsible AI Governance Framework


10. Acknowledgement
All employees must acknowledge this policy annually.
Use of company AI systems constitutes acceptance of this policy.
